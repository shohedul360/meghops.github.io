Who Needs CSPM

Cloud security posture management (CSPM) operates in three ways to provide protection to organizations. Firstly, it provides visibility by identifying misconfigurations, policy changes, and metadata related issues. Through a centralized console, CSPM helps manage these policies effectively. Secondly, CSPM manages and eliminates security risks by comparing cloud application configurations to industry benchmarks and fixing violations to accelerate the delivery process. By doing so, CSPM also reduces the risk of costly data breaches resulting from human error. Lastly, CSPMs proactively detect potential threats across the application development lifecycle by monitoring cloud environments for any inappropriate access and anomalies that may indicate malicious activity.

CSPM is necessary for organizations that want to automatically discover and remediate threats, compliance violations, and misconfigurations in public clouds. Misconfigurations in the cloud are one of the most common reasons for data breaches. CSPM tools provide peace of mind by ensuring the safety and security of cloud assets and configurations.

CSPMs are typically used by organizations that have adopted a cloud-first strategy and want to extend their cloud best practices to hybrid and multi-cloud environments. However, CSPMs are recommended for all enterprises, regardless of their size. Traditional security measures are not effective in the cloud, as there is no perimeter to protect, manual processes are not scalable, and the lack of centralization makes it difficult to achieve visibility. Hence, CSPMs are beneficial to all organizations.
